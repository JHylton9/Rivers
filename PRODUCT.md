# Product

## Register

product

## Users
This surface serves two overlapping audiences: analysts who need dependable access to river presets, dataset summaries, and selected feature details, and general viewers who need a calm introduction to the map without feeling overwhelmed. Most users arrive to explore Jamaica river data, orient themselves quickly, and then move into deeper inspection only when they want it.

## Product Purpose
This project presents Jamaica hydrography in an interactive map that balances approachability with analytical usefulness. Success looks like a sidebar that introduces the map clearly, leads people into the available views and dataset readouts, and keeps deeper controls accessible without making the experience feel cluttered or intimidating.

## Brand Personality
Grounded, dependable, refined. The tone should feel warm and high quality, with an understated premium character rather than something flashy, glossy, or synthetic.

## Anti-references
Avoid glossy SaaS-dashboard styling, luxury cliches, cluttered feed-like layouts, and heavy-handed technical portal aesthetics. The interface should not feel noisy, overly shaded, gimmicky, or overwhelming for casual viewers.

## Design Principles
1. Introduce first, deepen second: orient general viewers before asking them to interpret controls or data.
2. Preserve analytical confidence: deeper dataset views should stay obvious and trustworthy for repeat users.
3. Keep one visual vocabulary: buttons, sections, and supporting surfaces should feel related and intentional.
4. Use restraint to create quality: better spacing, typography, and hierarchy should do more work than decorative effects.
5. Make complexity optional: advanced-looking capability should be available without dominating the first impression.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Follow strong baseline accessibility practices with clear contrast, readable type, and visible control states. Motion should stay calm by default, with reduced-motion fallbacks respected from the start.
